What Koizora does

Three things, done with care.

A Children's Day app for grown-ups who want the day to mean something — without a spreadsheet, a group chat, and four reminder apps.

A koi for each child

Give every child a koinobori that grows with them. Each May, it gains a new band for the year — a living record they'll want to keep.

Track the climb

Height, first words, the small wins. Koizora turns a year of notes into one clear timeline you can scroll back through — the carp climbing its waterfall.

Plan May 5

Guest list, kashiwa-mochi order, who's bringing the kabuto. A shared plan the whole family can see, with gentle nudges as the day nears.

The timeline

One year, one ribbon at a time.

Open the app on any quiet evening and add a moment — a photo, a measured height, a line they said that you don't want to lose. Koizora threads them into a single rising stream, so the years don't blur together.

The tradition, kept

The fukinagashi at the top of the pole.

The five-colour streamer wards off bad luck for the children below. We kept it — as the ribbon that runs through Koizora, and as the thing your family actually flies. Order a real koinobori set from inside the app and we'll have it up your pole before Golden Week.
