May 5 · Kodomo no Hi

The avenue of carp.

Sixty poles rise from the riverbank. Each one carries a family — black carp for the father, red for the mother, and a smaller fish for every child in the household.

You arrive at dawn. The wind is just picking up.

Carp 180
Time 06:00
The river bend

Where the current turns.

The Sumida bends south here, and the carp swim against it. The water is clear, the stones are pale, and the morning light catches every ripple.

You can hear the falls before you see them.

River Sumida
Wind 3.2 m/s
Time 08:30
The waterfall

The climb that changes everything.

In the old tale, the carp that climbs the falls becomes a dragon. The wish, for a child, is not power — it is the courage to keep swimming when the water is strongest.

You stand at the base. The mist is cold.

Falls 12 m
Mist Heavy
Time 11:00
The dragon

From carp
to dragon.

The transformation is not a metaphor. It is what happens when a thing refuses to stop climbing. The dragon is the carp, still — only larger, only further up the falls.

You watch the procession at dusk. The children are invited to join.
