こどもの日

The day belongs to children

Every May, Japan raises carp streamers to the sky — a wish for courage, strength, and the freedom to rise. Scroll to fly through the world of Kodomo no Hi.

May 5th
鯉のぼり

Koinobori — carp that swim the sky

Each streamer represents a child in the family. The black carp leads, the red follows, and smaller ones trail behind — an avenue of hope stretching from gate to sky.

Courage

The river carries them forward

From the mountain springs to the valley floor, the river winds through the celebration. Families gather on its banks, sharing kashiwa-mochi and chimaki — rice cakes wrapped in oak and bamboo.

Tradition
竜門

The Dragon Gate

Legend says the carp that climbs the waterfall becomes a dragon. The falls are the test — the transformation is earned through persistence, not given. Every child who sees the streamer rise learns this story.

Growth
登

Rise

Every season, every streamer, every child — ascending. This is the spirit Nobori carries forward.
