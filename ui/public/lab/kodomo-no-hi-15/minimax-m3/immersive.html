May 5 · Kodomo no Hi

The morning the streamers rise.

五月五日。
鯉のぼりが空に昇る。

Every year on Children's Day, families across Japan raise koinobori — great carp streamers — on tall poles outside their homes. The carp swim upstream. They climb the waterfall. They become dragons.

Date May 5
Tradition 1,000+ yrs
Carp 5 per family
The Avenue

An avenue of carp, swimming skyward.

勇気と成長の
川を泳ぐ。

Each carp represents a family member. The black magoi for the father, the red for the mother, and smaller colored carp for each child. The wind catches them. They ripple. They climb.

Magoi Father
Hi-goi Mother
Children Each color
The River

The river bends. The carp follow.

川が曲がり、
鯉が続く。

The koinobori fly above a river that winds through the hills toward the waterfall. The carp don't just decorate the sky — they swim. They follow the current upstream, toward the place where water becomes flight.

Direction Upstream
Wind From the east
Season Early summer
The Waterfall

The falls. The climb. The becoming.

滝を登り、
龍になる。

At the end of the river, the waterfall. The carp that climbs it becomes a dragon — the ancient symbol of courage, strength, and transformation. Every child who flies a koinobori is wished that climb.

Climb Courage
Transform Dragon
Wish Growth
