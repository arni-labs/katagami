The festival

A day for courage, growth, and the carp that climbs the waterfall.

Kodomo no Hi — Children's Day — has been celebrated in Japan for over a thousand years. On May 5th, families fly koinobori — great carp streamers — on tall poles outside their homes. Each carp represents a family member: the black magoi for the father, the red for the mother, and smaller colored carp for each child.

The carp swims upstream. It climbs the waterfall. It becomes a dragon. The festival honors courage and growth — the wish that every child will have the strength to rise.
