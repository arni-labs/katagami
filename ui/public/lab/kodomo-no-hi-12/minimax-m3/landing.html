The planning surface

One product. Built around the day.

Three surfaces — plan, send, remember — wired into a single shared timeline so every role knows where they stand.

01 · Plan

A timeline the family actually reads.

A shared schedule anchored to dawn. The carp rise at sunrise; breakfast is at 8; guests arrive at 10. Every member of the family gets a slice of the day in the language they understand — to-do, ritual, or both.

  • Role-aware reminders — grandfather gets the prayer list; father gets the hoist checklist.
  • Auto-translated invites for relatives abroad, with traditional and modern phrasings.
  • Weather-aware: a gust forecast moves the carp indoors automatically.
02 · Send

Invitations that look like invitations.

Printable, postable, sendable. Each invite carries the family's name and the date in vertical kanji-friendly layout, with RSVP that updates the headcount in real time. No PDF template graveyard.

  • Vertical or horizontal layouts — choose by wall or by envelope.
  • One-tap RSVP — guests reply in their language; you see it in yours.
  • Printed keepsake mode: archive the day's invite forever.
03 · Remember

A memory book that grows with the carp.

Kogyo saves the year's celebration as a printed keepsake — every photo, every role, every guest. The eldest's height next to the streamer pole. The kabuto helmet on the second child. The grandfather's prayer.

  • Year-on-year growth charts — the carp and the child, side by side.
  • Print-ready PDF keepsake shipped within a week of May 5.
  • Family archive: searchable by year, role, and ritual.
