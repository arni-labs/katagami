What it does

From carp pole to keepsake, in one place.

KOGANE replaces the clipboard, the group chat, and the spreadsheet. Plan the display, run the day, capture the memories, and ship the thank-yous — without losing the warmth of the festival.

01 / Display

Plan the pole, pole by pole.

Drag-and-drop layout for the yard display. Koinobori, fukinagashi, kabuto, kashiwa-mochi — every element placed on a single shared canvas, with wind direction and sightlines baked in.

02 / Run the day

The morning of, without the panic.

A live timeline for May 5 itself: who's setting up, when the carp goes up, who's reading the祝福, and what the kids are doing between events. Updates push to every parent's phone.

03 / Keep it

A memory book that actually gets made.

Every photo, every note, every child's wish — auto-assembled into a printed Kodomo no Hi album by the end of the week. No one has to remember to do it.

The dashboard

One screen for the whole celebration.

The KOGANE dashboard is the operational view for the day: who's confirmed, what's shipped, what's still missing. Built for the parent volunteer who doesn't have time to learn a new tool.

  • 1
    Live RSVP count — families confirmed, kids attending, dietary notes, all in one column.
  • 2
    Supply chain — carp streamers, kashiwa-mochi, calligraphy ink, kabuto helmets. Tracked from order to door.
  • 3
    Weather & wind — a 5-day forecast for your exact pole location, with a "go / hold" recommendation for May 5 morning.
