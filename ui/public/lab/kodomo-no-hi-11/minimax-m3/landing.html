節句 · Tango no sekku

A set is a household that flies together.

The legend is the carp: it swims the river, climbs the falls, and crosses the threshold into the dragon. On the fifth of May each family hangs that journey on a pole outside their house — one black magoi, one red higoi, and three smaller child streamers. The wind does the rest.

Koinobori studio dyes each streamer by hand, paints each crest from a family-supplied mon, and ships the assembled set in a numbered wooden crate. You hang it; the wind scales the falls.

Every set we ship is one magoi, one higoi, and three child streamers.

工程 · The five-week process

From your 紋 to the wind, in five weeks.

Every set is a numbered commission. We do not print-on-demand; we dye, paint and assemble by hand, on a calendar that closes on the last day of February.

5weeks lead time
STEP 01

Design the mon

紋を写す

You send us the family crest — a photograph of the old set, a sketch on washi, or just a description. We redraw it for the pole.

Week 1
STEP 02

Cut the silk

絹を裁つ

Hand-cut panels of Habotai silk, one panel per streamer. The five streamers go 5m / 4m / 2m / 1.6m / 1.2m — the standard taper.

Week 2
STEP 03

Dye the silk

染める

Indigo, vermilion, moss, river blue, and a gold-leaf finish. Each panel dipped five times — the depth is what makes the colour hold in wind.

Week 3
STEP 04

Paint the crest

家紋を描く

The family mon painted by hand across all five streamers. Two passes — first ink, then gold leaf for the kinka.

Week 4
STEP 05

Hang and ship

揚げる

Bamboo pole cut to the height of your house, streamers mounted, set packed in a numbered crate, dispatched by truck.

Week 5

申込 · How to order

Three steps, one form.

  1. 01 Send your mon. A photograph of your existing set, a sketch, or a written description. The studio redraws it for the new pole.
  2. 02 Confirm dimensions. Height of your eaves, width of the yard, number of child streamers. We size the bamboo and the silk.
  3. 03 Pay the deposit. 30% on order, 70% on dispatch. Standard lead time is five weeks; express is five days for an existing mon.

工房より · A note from the studio

Three people, one dye floor, eleven sets a year.

Koinobori studio is run by Akiko Mori, her brother Daiki, and one apprentice named Yuna. They have been dyeing silk in the same wood-frame house in Kanazawa since 1968. The dye floor still uses the same four vats their father built.

They do not print, they do not mass-dye, and they do not take commissions after February. The studio answers the phone on weekday mornings and on Saturday until noon.

— Akiko Mori Director, Koinobori studio · 第三代目
