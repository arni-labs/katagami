Programs

Three currents, one river

Each program is a stream that feeds the same goal: children who can think, make, and lead with confidence.

Courage Workshops

Weekly live sessions where children present original work to peers — building the muscle of speaking up, receiving feedback, and iterating in public.

Curiosity Labs

Self-paced investigations into real questions — why do rivers bend, how do bridges stand, what makes music move us — with mentors who ask better questions.

Craft Studios

Hands-on making — from woodworking to code to ceramics — where children ship real artifacts. Every term ends with an exhibition, not a test.

The Legend

The carp that climbed the waterfall became a dragon

In Japanese tradition, the koi fish swims against the current — a symbol of perseverance. At the Dragon Gate waterfall, the strongest koi transforms into a dragon. Nobori takes its name from this ascent: every child has a waterfall to climb, and the right environment turns effort into transformation.
