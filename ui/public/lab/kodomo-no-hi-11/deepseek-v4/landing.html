Our Craft

Silk, washi, and three generations of handwork

Every Nobori streamer begins in our Nishijin workshop, where silk is stretched on hundred-year-old looms and painted by hand with natural mineral pigments. A single large koinobori takes eleven days from loom to sky.

The Nishijin workshop

Our master dyer, Tanaka-sensei, has been painting carp scales for forty-seven years. He mixes his own vermillion from safflower and cinnabar, and his indigo from fermented sukumo leaves — the same recipes his grandfather used when the workshop opened in 1928.

Each streamer passes through twelve hands before it ships: the silk weaver, the pattern cutter, the dye master, the scale painter, the eye painter, the fin assembler, the pole fitter, and five quality inspectors who check every seam under morning light.

The Story

Why the carp swims upstream

Kodomo no Hi began as Tango no Sekku over a thousand years ago — a day when families prayed for their sons' strength and success. The koinobori tradition draws from a Chinese legend: a carp that swam up the Yellow River's Dragon Gate waterfall was transformed into a dragon. Fly a carp streamer, the saying goes, and your child will grow with the courage to climb any waterfall.
