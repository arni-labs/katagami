こどもの日 / Kodomo no Hi
鯉のぼり
The wind lifts them.

We raise the poles along the river so the children can watch their banners climb. This is the story of the koi that reaches the falls and becomes the dragon.

The avenue • 鯉のぼり
Black for the father.
Red for the mother.
Blue, green, pink for the children.

Every banner is stitched by hand. When the wind turns, the whole family turns to watch.

The river • 川
The current is steady.
The banners follow the water.

We walk the bank. The poles stand in the grass like sentinels. The koi swim in air.

The climb • 滝
At the waterfall the water strikes the rock and rises again as mist.

Here the koi gathers its strength. The family stands silent. One child whispers the name of the one who will climb.

竜 • Ryū
The koi that reaches the top
becomes a dragon.

On this day we tell the children: the climb is difficult. The wind does not always help. But the family waits on the bank, and the banners are already flying.

May 5 — for every child who will one day stand taller than the poles.
