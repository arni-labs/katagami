May 5

Kodomo no Hi

Children’s Day is a national celebration of strength, growth, and family. Across Japan, tall poles line rivers and gardens. Black for fathers, red for mothers, blue, green and pink for the children — the koinobori rise and fill with wind.

On the fifth day of the fifth month, we honour every child’s climb and every family that lifts them.

The story we raise

The Ascent

The carp swims upstream against the current. At the waterfall it gathers everything it has and leaps. If it succeeds, it becomes a dragon.

Koinobori tell that story to every child who watches the banners move. The climb is hard. The wind is constant. The family stands together on the bank.

This is not decoration. It is a promise made visible.

The waterfall
Where the koi becomes the dragon.

We place the tallest pole nearest the water. The black streamer flies highest. The smaller ones follow. The children run beneath them until the sun drops.
