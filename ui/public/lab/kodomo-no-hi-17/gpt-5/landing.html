The day at full height

Carp streamers in the May wind

The route begins under a high line of koinobori. Each streamer marks a family wish: strength in the current, resilience in weather, and a child carried forward by the whole town.

01

Welcome gate

Volunteers tie new streamers to the opening line and hand out the day's route sheet before the first drum call.

02

Sky corridor

A long walk beneath blue, green, and hot koi bands, with pauses for family photographs and small dedication tags.

03

Dusk lift

The final raising happens when the light turns low and the streamers cut clean shapes against the river.

Air made visible

The graphic system follows the same idea: long bands reveal motion, direction, and gathering without crowding the day.

Customs with clean hands and bright edges

Kodomo no Hi is tactile without becoming messy: folded paper, oak leaves, helmets, baths scented with iris, and wishes spoken into clear air.

Kabuto folding

Make a paper helmet at the long table, then stamp the lining with the year's festival mark and a family name.

Oak leaf wishes

Kashiwa leaves carry the wish for continuity. Guests write a short note, tie it, and leave space for the next family.

Iris water

Fresh shobu is bundled at the garden station, connecting the day to courage, health, and the scent of early summer.

Festival pulse

A simple day with enough structure to feel generous: arrive, walk, make, eat, listen, and return to the streamers when the light changes.
