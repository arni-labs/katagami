Public festival
May 5

Morning streamers, midday performances, late afternoon food tables, and a green river walk built for families and friends.

The rising

Koinobori catch the clean wind.

The day begins with poles along the water. Carp-streamers lift in lanes of blue, green, and coral above the tents, making the riverbank feel taller than the city around it.

Morning

Raise the poles

Volunteers pull the streamers into place before the first families arrive. The route stays wide, white, and open so people can look up without stopping the flow.

Midday

Green shade

Iris bundles, bamboo leaves, and picnic cloths make the middle of the festival fresh instead of crowded. The brightest colour is kept for wayfinding and special moments.

Evening

One hot mark

Coral marks the shared toast, the final taiko piece, and the family photograph hour. It appears rarely, so it lands with confidence.

Customs in public

The old day stays specific.

Nobori keeps the celebration concrete: food you can hold, leaves you can smell, songs you can hear, and streamers you can watch from the grass.

Koinobori

Carp for strength

Each streamer is hung high enough to move freely, with open ground below for viewing and photographs.

Iris leaves

Fresh bundles

Green stalls prepare iris bundles for the bath ritual and for the festival's scent table.

Mochi table

Kashiwa and chimaki

The food line is treated like a calm counter, not a queue crush, with clear pickup windows.

Wish cards

Names in the wind

Families write wishes and fasten them near the poles before the afternoon performance.

River walk

A route that breathes.

The celebration is arranged as lanes, not boxes: look up at the streamers, step sideways for food, then return to the green path for workshops and performances.

Festival rhythm

Clear arrivals, slow middle, bright finish.

The companion keeps live details dense, but the welcome page stays generous. Both surfaces use the same wind-band logic and the same three accents.

The feeling

Not a party theme. A day people enter.

Adults can make it, host it, and be proud of it. Children are honoured without turning the page into a toy box.

Civic

Public, not private

Wide lanes, real programme times, resting grass, and clear meeting points make the day usable for a crowd.

Graphic

Poster energy

Big type, hard crops, and highlighter fields give the festival a confident masthead from arrival to closing.

Fresh

Green air

The ground stays bright and light. Accents cut through like clear daylight, not pastel decoration.
