Wind for May

May 5

Koinobori rise from the river lawn at 9:00, workshops start at 10:30, and the evening wind parade closes at 18:40.

The river lifts first

Fukinagashi opens the day with space and direction. The field stays white and clear while the streamers carry the colour. Sky, leaf, and vermilion behave like highlighter marks across the celebration.

Morning hoist

Every carp has room to breathe

Families tie their streamers along the river rail, then the civic crew raises the longest line into the open wind. The design keeps that same discipline: wide air, deliberate marks, no clutter.

Customs with clear edges

The day is gentle, but the composition is confident. Each custom gets a strong place in the field, with tone shifts instead of boxes and meaningful colour rather than decoration.

01

Koinobori line

Carp streamers are raised for courage, resilience, and the wish that children move through life with force and grace.

02

Iris leaves

Fresh shobu leaves appear at the wash station and craft tables, bringing the early-summer scent into the public square.

03

Kashiwa mochi

Oak-leaf sweets are served after the noon greeting, a compact ritual of continuity that belongs to the whole field.

04

Kabuto display

A small armour display sits beside the archive tent, framed as craft and memory rather than spectacle.

Clean food, bright pause

The menu keeps the same grown-up festival voice: simple seasonal food, clear queue marks, shade seating, and enough room for families to settle without losing the rhythm of the programme.
