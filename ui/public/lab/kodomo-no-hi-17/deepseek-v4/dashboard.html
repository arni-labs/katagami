Visitor's Guide

What to bring, what to know

Whether you are hosting at home or joining a community celebration, a little preparation makes the day seamless.

Dress for the Weather

Early May in Japan is mild — 18–24°C. Light layers, comfortable shoes for park walking. Many families wear traditional attire: children in jinbei, parents in casual kimono or yukata.

Arrive Early

The koinobori raising at dawn is the day's most photogenic moment — the morning light through the fabric streamers is unforgettable. Community events fill up by mid-morning.

Bring a Picnic

Kashiwa mochi and chimaki are the traditional foods, but any shared meal works. A blanket, bento boxes, and a thermos of green tea. The park picnic is the heart of the day.

Learn the Greeting

"Kodomo no Hi omedetō" — congratulations on Children's Day. A simple phrase that connects you to the families around you. The festival is warm and welcoming to visitors.
