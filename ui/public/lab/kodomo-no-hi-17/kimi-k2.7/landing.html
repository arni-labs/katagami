Kodomo no Hi — 5 May

The streamers are rising.

A day of clear light, fresh green, and carp climbing the wind. Gather for the Koinobori Festival in Satsuki Park — music, crafts, food, and the feeling of early summer.

Plan your day Discover the festival

01 / The carp streamers

Koinobori rise against the May sky.

From the black carp father at the top to the red and blue children below, each streamer carries a wish for strength, health, and a bright future.

A streamer for every child.

Families hang koinobori outside their homes and along the riverbanks. At Satsuki Park, more than two hundred streamers fly above the meadow — a single black carp, red carp, and blue carp for each family.

  • Black carp for the father, strength and leadership
  • Red carp for the mother, warmth and care
  • Blue carp for each child, hope and ambition

02 / Customs

Traditions that feel like summer.

Kodomo no Hi is a national holiday in Japan, but the festival here is open to everyone. The customs are simple, joyful, and made to share.

Kabuto crafting

Fold paper samurai helmets at the craft tent. The kabuto symbolises protection and courage for children.

Iris baths

Shobu-yoku iris baths are prepared near the tea house. The fragrance is said to ward off illness and bring good health.

Kashiwa mochi

Sweet rice cakes wrapped in oak leaves are shared at midday. The leaf represents prosperity and family continuity.

03 / Food

Flavours of the festival.

The food stalls open at eleven. Expect kashiwa mochi, chimaki wrapped in bamboo leaves, chilled barley tea, and freshly grilled ayu from the river.

Everything is served on reusable dishes. Bring your own bottle for free refills of iced mugicha.

04 / The feeling

A day built for families.

The festival is free, open to all ages, and designed to move at the pace of children. There are quiet corners for nursing, shaded seating, and water stations throughout the park.

Come as you are.

Whether you are tracing your first carp streamer or bringing grandchildren to the meadow, the day belongs to you. Volunteers in sky-blue sashes are stationed at every entrance.

  • Free entry and craft materials
  • Accessible paths and seating
  • Quiet hour from 9:00 to 10:00
