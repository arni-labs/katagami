Kodomo no Hi · May 5

The carp rise, and the sky fills with hope.

Satsuki is a public celebration of children — a day when families raise koinobori to the clear early-summer wind, share kashiwa-mochi, and wish the next generation the strength to swim upstream.

01 The Rise

Cloth carp that swim in the wind.

A koinobori is a carp made of cloth, hoisted on a tall pole. When the wind catches it, the carp fills and climbs — black carp for the father, red for the mother, blue for the child, and one more for each sibling. The streamer does not fly; it ascends, the way a strong child grows.

02 The Customs

Wishes for strength, drawn in objects.

Every object on the day carries a wish: that the child grow brave, resilient, and kind. The customs are old, and they are made new each May.

Armour

Kabuto & Gogatsu-ningyo

Families display a kabuto helmet and a May doll of a young warrior — not for war, but for the wish that a child meet difficulty the way armour meets a blow: whole, and standing.

Bath

Shobu-yu

On the evening of the fifth, families steep shobu — sweet-flag iris leaves — in the bath. The iris's sword-shaped leaves carry the wish for health through the heat of the summer to come.

Carp

Koinobori no Aki

Children write a single wish on a paper carp and tie it where the wind will find it. The wish is simple, and private, and it belongs to the child alone — the wind does the reading.

03 The Food

Leaves that carry the sweet.

Two sweets define the day, and both come wrapped in a leaf — because the wish is for a child to grow the way a leaf grows: from something that was itself once green.

04 The Feeling
A child does not become strong because you tell them to be. They become strong because, once a year, the whole street lifts a cloth fish into the wind and watches it climb.
— from the Satsuki festival programme, 2026

What the day asks of you

  • Arrive early. The wind is steadiest before noon, and the great carp is raised at 09:30.
  • Bring a wish. Paper carp are free at the children's tent — one per child.
  • Stay for the bath. Shobu-yu opens at 16:00; the leaves are gathered from the park.
05 Gather

Reserve a pole for your family.

Poles are limited and assigned by lottery. Register your family by April 28; we will write to confirm your pole number and the colour of your carp.

Satsuki

A public celebration of Kodomo no Hi, held each May 5 at Midori Park on the riverside grounds. Free entry; poles by lottery.
