Why paper

Every other star app hands you a black screen. The sky deserves a plate.

A glowing dark UI kills your night vision and flattens the sky to dots. The historical answer was better: a ruled plate, objects placed by coordinate, brightness drawn as contrast. Lumen reads like that atlas — and stays out of your dark-adapted eyes.

The structural idea

One motif: the ruling.

i

Ruled, never bordered.

Every screen sits on the coordinate grid; content is framed by engraver's corner ticks. No lines box anything in.

ii

Hierarchy is magnitude.

The most important element is the brightest — full ink and the single amber mark. Supporting content dims, like fainter stars.

iii

The night gets one pane.

Exactly one dark indigo surface per view. The page stays light, so the sky doesn't blind you at the eyepiece.
