# Brief — identical for every model

Design a brand-new design language for **Lumen**, a fictional consumer stargazing
& astronomy companion app.

## The product

Lumen helps amateur astronomers and curious night-sky watchers plan observation
sessions and keep an observation log. Core jobs:

- **Plan tonight** — given the user's location, show what's visible: planets, the
  Moon phase, notable deep-sky objects, ISS passes, meteor showers, and the best
  observing window (clear-sky + darkness + object altitude).
- **The sky atlas** — browse objects (planets, constellations, nebulae, clusters,
  galaxies) with coordinates (RA / Dec), magnitude, rise/set times, and a short
  field description.
- **Observation log** — record what you saw: object, date/time, equipment,
  seeing/transparency conditions, sketch or notes, a rating.
- **Conditions** — light pollution (Bortle scale), cloud cover forecast, Moon
  interference, the night's astronomical-twilight window.

## What to produce

1. **`DESIGN.md`** — the full Lumen design language.
2. **`landing.html`** — the marketing landing page for Lumen.
3. **`dashboard.html`** — the in-app dashboard: "tonight's sky" as the home view,
   with the visible-objects list, the observing-window timeline, conditions, and a
   slice of the observation log. Real data density — coordinates, magnitudes,
   times, a table — not swatches.

## Notes

- Invent believable real content (actual object names, plausible RA/Dec,
  magnitudes, times, place names). Don't use lorem ipsum.
- The subject is the night sky — but the contract requires **light mode**. Design a
  beautiful *light* astronomy product. This tension is intentional; resolve it with
  taste, not by breaking the contract.
- Give Lumen a distinctive structural motif of its own — do not imitate any existing
  Katagami language.
