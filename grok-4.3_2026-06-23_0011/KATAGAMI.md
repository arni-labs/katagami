# Nobori

Nobori is a bright, adult Kodomo no Hi design language for **Nobori Atlas**, a product that helps museums, city teams, cultural programs, and brand studios launch koinobori installations with the care of a press edition and the clarity of an operations dashboard.

## Identity

The language turns the koinobori’s rise into a product system. White space is the sky. Large ink blocks are the masthead. Electric sky-blue, fresh leaf-green, and hot coral are used like highlighter marks for action, readiness, and urgency. The pages stay sleek, clean, and grown-up: early summer optimism without childish clutter.

## Signature mechanic

**Wind-lift composition**: modules climb through offset vertical lanes. One large image, number, or press block leads; smaller operational details follow below it. The page feels like streamers catching wind, but every decision remains usable and product-driven.

## Product world

Nobori Atlas supports:

- Installation planning for May 5 programs.
- Editioned koinobori textile releases.
- Venue approvals and wind-window reviews.
- Community launch pages and collection dashboards.
- Adult-facing cultural campaigns that need hope, speed, and restraint.

## Published artifacts

- `DESIGN.md` defines the complete language system, tokens, controls, states, component recipes, and sources.
- `embodiment.html` shows Nobori Atlas as a canonical product command surface.
- `landing.html` is the public marketing page with a single full-bleed generated hero image.
- `dashboard.html` is a live-feeling operations dashboard.
- `media/` contains cohesive generated imagery used by the product surfaces.
- `components.md` and `preview-shots.json` provide shadcn-oriented component and scene contracts required by Katagami taste rules.

## Implementation notes

Every visible page avoids token swatches and specimen framing. The product surfaces show the system in use rather than explaining it. Controls use one shared `--control-height` token, focus is visible, radii stay within the Katagami allowed set, surfaces separate by tone instead of borders, and imagery comes from an image model rather than hand-drawn SVG.
