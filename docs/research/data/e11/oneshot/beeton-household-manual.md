TO REBUILD THE VILLAGE BRIDGE AFTER A FLOOD.

MATERIALS.--40 tons of dressed stone, 12 oak baulks each 20 feet long, 8 bushels of lime, 3 cart-loads of sharp gravel, 1 master mason, 6 steady labourers, and the old plan kept in the vestry chest.

Mode.--The flood having carried away three arches in a single August night, wait for the river to fall and the bed to dry before a stone is laid. Clear the ruin first; save every squared block the water has spared, for these are cheaper than the quarry. Set the centring for the arches upon firm piles. Raise the springing a full foot above the old flood-mark, that the next spate may pass beneath the bridge and not against it. Bed each stone well in lime, and let no course be hurried. Some of the parish will have the bridge built again exactly as it stood, from love of the thing they knew; others will have it built as it ought to have been, higher and wider for the market carts. Weigh both counsels, and give the river the last word, for it has argued the longest.

Time.--Two months for the work; a whole winter of talk before a spade is lifted.

Average cost, sixty pounds, the saved stone and the borrowed centring reckoned in.

Seasonable in late summer, when the water runs low and the days are long for masons.

Note.--Build once and build high; a bridge set back to its old height is a bridge already half lost.
