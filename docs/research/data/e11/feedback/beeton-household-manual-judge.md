# Revision feedback — Beeton household manual

Judged by ear against the corpus (the bottle-jack receipt) and the known-good clock replica.

- The Mode section abandons instruction and turns essayist. "Some of the parish will have the bridge built again exactly as it stood, from love of the thing they knew; others will have it built as it ought to have been... Weigh both counsels, and give the river the last word, for it has argued the longest." Beeton never narrates where she can instruct. When she moralizes it is a single homely maxim tossed off mid-procedure ("Spare the rod, and spoil the child" → "Spare the basting, and spoil the meat"), and she is straight back to the joint. Here the reflection on parish sentiment swells into the climax of the receipt. Cut it to one crisp aside and keep the imperative marching.

- "Give the river the last word, for it has argued the longest" is a literary personification that Beeton would never write. Her register is plain, brisk, transactional — she wastes no morning on rhetoric. Strike the conceit; state the fact (build to clear the flood-mark) and move on.

- The procedure is too thin for Beeton's hand. The corpus packs a single apparatus with dense, sequenced craft-detail — the spring, the brass cylinder, the winding, the hook, the chain, the wheel, the dripping-pan beneath, the objection to the screen. The draft gives only a handful of general directives ("Set the centring... Raise the springing... Bed each stone well in lime"). Enumerate the real steps of the work in order, with their reasons attached, the way she explains why the meat-screen beats the enclosed jack.

- "Remark: one sentence of context" is the standing rule, and the draft breaks it — the whole parish-politics passage is several sentences of context where the form allows one. Compress the human note to a single line if you keep it at all.

- Sentiment keeps leaking in: "from love of the thing they knew," "as it ought to have been." Beeton records cost, time, and method without wistfulness. Keep the emotional temperature at zero and let the quantities carry the weight.

- The imperative stretch runs at one uniform medium length. Beeton swings hard — a very short flat order ("IN STIRRING THE FIRE... the dripping-pan should always be drawn back") set against a long winding compound. Drop in a couple of blunt one-clause commands to break the even gait.

- What lands: the receipt skeleton (title, MATERIALS, Mode, Time, Average cost, Seasonable, Note), list-before-method, and the closing Note as a maxim ("Build once and build high...") are true to her. Preserve those; the fix is tonal, not structural.
