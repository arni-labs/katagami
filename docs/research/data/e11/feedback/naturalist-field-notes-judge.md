# Revision feedback — Naturalist field notes (Darwin + White blend)

Judged by ear against the corpus (Darwin's beetles-at-sea) and the known-good parish-clock replica, which closes on White's swallow analogy.

- This is a blend of Darwin and White, and White has gone missing. His fingerprint is the natural-history analogy — human habit read through the bird or the plant ("as swallows will seek the barn whose eaves have been stopped against them," in the very replica this contract ships). The draft observes a village's custom but never once reaches into nature for the comparison. It reads as all-Darwin travelogue with none of the parson-naturalist who sees the swallow in the man. Give it one true White turn: the villagers clinging to the old shape the way some creature returns to a habit outlived by its cause.

- "A lesson written in the one stone the flood could not carry" is a poetic flourish the naturalist voice would not dress a specimen in. State the observed fact flat — the arch whose pier stood square to the current survived, the two set askew did not — and let the reader draw the moral. The corpus earns its inferences by understatement ("it appeared to me most probable"); it never varnishes them.

- The comparisons that are here are all engineering and social ("as I have seen done upon the larger rivers to the south"). That is Darwin's half. The move "compare the specimen to the known" is meant to range across nature, not just across bridges. The specificity in the draft is entirely of human counsel; a field naturalist would also fix the bank, the season, the water itself in measured particulars.

- The piece is one unbroken paragraph of long, comma-laced sentences at nearly the same length. Both authors punctuate their long analytic sentences with short flat beats — Darwin's "Captain Cook observed it, as did more lately Captain King of the Adventure." The draft has only "Which counsel will prevail I am not yet able to say" to break the tide. Set a few short declaratives against the long ones so the rhythm swings rather than rolls.

- The register never shifts. The contract flexes between the immediate journal entry and the systematic summary; the draft stays in one retrospective, summarizing key throughout. A moment of present, close observation — something seen and measured on the spot — would earn the reflection that follows it.

- What lands: the dated-and-placed opening, the measured flood-mark ("seven feet and some inches... by the mark cut upon the mill-post"), the honest closing admission, and the aphorism compressing observation into a law ("restore what they have lost in the very shape of its losing") are genuinely in the corpus's key. Keep them; the gap is the absent White and the one aestheticized line.
