TO REBUILD THE VILLAGE BRIDGE AFTER A FLOOD.

MATERIALS.--40 tons of dressed stone, 12 oak baulks each 20 feet long, 8 bushels of lime, 3 cart-loads of sharp gravel, 1 master mason, 6 steady labourers, and the old plan kept in the vestry chest.

Mode.--The flood having carried away three arches in a single August night, wait until the river falls and the bed is dry before a stone is laid. Clear the ruin first. Save every squared block the water has spared, and set them apart, since these are sounder than the new and cheaper than the quarry. Sound each old pier to its foundation with an iron bar, and pull down whatever the current has loosened, that no good work be set upon rotten. Fix the centring for the arches upon firm piles, and wedge it true. Raise the springing a full foot above the old flood-mark, that the next spate pass beneath the bridge and not against it. Bed each stone well in lime. Let no course be hurried, and turn no arch until the course below it has set hard. Some will have it built as it stood; hear them, and build it higher.

Time.--Two months for the work; a whole winter of talk before a spade is lifted.

Average cost, sixty pounds, the saved stone and the borrowed centring reckoned in.

Seasonable in summer, when the water runs low and the days are long.

Note.--Build once and build high; a bridge set back to its old height is a bridge already half lost.
