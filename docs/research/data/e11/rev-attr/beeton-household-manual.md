TO REBUILD THE VILLAGE BRIDGE AFTER A FLOOD.

MATERIALS.--40 tons of dressed stone, 12 oak baulks each 20 feet long, 8 bushels of lime, 3 cart-loads of sharp gravel, 1 master mason, 6 steady labourers, and the old plan kept in the vestry chest.

Mode.--The flood having carried off three arches in one night, wait for the river to fall and the bed to dry before a stone is laid. Clear the ruin first. Save every squared block the water has spared, and dress them afresh; such stone is cheaper, and sooner cut, than the quarry's. This ground being firm, set the centring upon piles, which bear the weight till the lime is hard. Raise the springing a full foot above the old flood-mark, that the next spate may pass beneath and not drive into it. Bed each stone in lime, and fill every joint with mortar; let no course be hurried. Some of the parish will have it raised exactly as it stood, from love of the thing they knew; others will have it set higher and wider, to take the market carts. Weigh both counsels, and give the river the last word, for it has argued the longest.

Time.--Two months at the work; a whole winter of talk before a spade is lifted.

Average cost, sixty pounds, the saved stone and borrowed centring reckoned in.

Seasonable in late summer, when the water runs low and the days are long.

Note.--Build once and build high; a bridge set back to its old height is a bridge half lost.
