TO KEEP A LIGHTHOUSE LOG.

MATERIALS.--1 bound ledger, ruled; 1 steel pen; ink, black; 1 lamp, trimmed nightly; forty years of attention.

Mode.--Enter each night, without fail, the hour, the wind, the state of the sea, and the measure of oil consumed. Write plainly, that another may read it after you. Rule a second column if you choose, and in it set down the same day as it moved you; but never suffer the second column to delay the first, and never leave the light to the fancy while the fact goes unwritten. Time.--A quarter of an hour nightly; less for the plain record, more for the embellished. Cost.--A penny of ink the month, and no idleness whatever.

The old keeper of the point kept to this rule for forty years, and at his death both columns were found entire, the plain and the fine agreeing in every particular of the weather. The town now disputes whether the finer entries should be printed. Print the plain: they are the record, and belong to the coast. Preserve the fine: they are the man, and belong to his people. Do not confound the one office with the other. A log, like an account-book, is worth nothing if it flatters and everything if it is true; and there is no reason a truthful book may not also be a handsome one, provided always the truth is written down first.
