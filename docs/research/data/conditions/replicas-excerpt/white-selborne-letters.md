Your account of the lighthouse keeper's doubled logbook is very curious; and strange it is that a man so hemmed about with duty should find leisure to write every entry a second time, and in so much finer a strain! It pleases me to find that the book is preserved entire, and especially as you assure me that no page wants its fellow; for records of this sort are so few that every such discovery is a great acquisition. I never was informed before that keepers were given to this practice. You admit, I find, that the embellished hand is the same as the plain, and I think you may be secure that you are right; for the townsfolk took very particular pains to compare them, and had some fair witnesses among the old people, who remembered the man. The parson, I hear, would have it a kind of devotion, a psalm set down beside the tide-table; but I am more inclined to think it the natural overflow of a solitary mind, much as the caged bird will sing though none be by to hear it. You will, no doubt, give it its proper place in your history of the parish. De Réaumur has somewhere described a like case among the keepers of the French coast; but still I am pleased you have found one nearer home, for the reason I gave in my last.
