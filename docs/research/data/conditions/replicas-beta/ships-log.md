The twelfth of March, and my first week in the tower. Wind light from the west, glass steady, the lamp lit at six and drawing clean. Among the old keeper's things I found his logs, forty years of them, and settled to read a little each night when the light was set. Every day in every volume is entered twice. First the plain line — wind, sea, the hour of lighting, the hour of dousing, the stores got in — and under it the same day again in a fuller hand, the fog given a voice, a gale worked up out of a fresh breeze. He had stood forty winters alone up here, and a man so placed will make company of his own weather. I read till the oil was low and could not say I blamed him. The town has heard of it now. Some call him touched, and some would have the fine passages copied out and framed in the hall. For my part I hold that both hands were true, the one for the board of trade and the one for himself. I have set his books back on their shelf and will keep my own log single, as I was taught. Wind holding fair at nightfall; all well in the light.
