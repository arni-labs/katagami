TO KEEP THE LIGHTHOUSE LOG.

MATERIALS.—1 bound ledger of 400 leaves, 2 pens, 1 bottle of oak-gall ink, 1 lantern for the night watch, and 1 steady hand willing to write the same day twice.

Mode.—Rule each leaf into two columns, the left broad, the right broader. In the left, enter the plain facts as they stand: the hour of lighting, the strength of the wind, the ships sighted, the oil consumed. Write nothing there that the eye has not seen. In the right, set down the same day a second time, but freely, giving the fog its silver and the gull its errand, as the heart moves you. Keep both hands small and legible, that a stranger may read either. Bind the year's leaves at Christmas, and lay the volume by.

Time.—Ten minutes for the plain column each night; a quarter of an hour for the other, according to the weather of the mind.

Average cost, threepence a week in ink, the lantern-oil not reckoned, being spent already upon the light.

Seasonable through the whole year, but fullest in winter, when the nights are long and a keeper has hours to spare for the second telling.

Note.—Should the book be found and read after forty years, expect the town to quarrel a fortnight over whether the man was a liar or a poet, and to settle nothing.
