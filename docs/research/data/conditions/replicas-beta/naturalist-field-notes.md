October 3rd.--The harbour light stands on a spit of grey basalt, and its keeper being lately dead, the parish desired his forty years of logs examined before storing. On opening the first volume I remarked at once a peculiarity that runs through the whole series without a single exception. Each day is entered twice. The first entry is bare: the direction of the wind, the state of the glass, the hours of lighting and extinguishing the lamp, the stores received (these last set down to the pound). Beneath it the same day is written a second time, but in a fuller hand, the fog described as a living thing, the sea endowed with a temper it cannot be said to possess. I counted, in a single year taken at random, one hundred and forty such doubled entries, and the practice does not slacken with age. It bears some likeness to the habit of certain solitary birds, which will sing a full song where no rival and no mate can hear them. Whether he wrote his second entry for want of company, for a private pleasure in the doing, or from some cause I have not hit upon, I cannot determine. The townspeople are divided. Some hold him to have been disordered by his solitude; others, that a considerable writer passed his life unregarded among them. On the evidence I decline either conclusion, and note only that both records agree in every fact of wind and weather, the one being plain where the other is dressed.
