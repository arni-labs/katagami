The mistress of a house may learn something useful from the lighthouse keeper whose book has lately been the talk of the coast. His method was plain and worth the copying. Every night, for forty years, he entered his account twice over. One entry he made plainly. He wrote the hour, the wind, the state of the oil, the passing of any ship, in the fewest words that would serve. The other he made purely for pleasure. Here the same night was set down in full colour, the wind given a temper, the lamp a kind of courage, the dark water a watching face. Nothing was wasted, and nothing was hurried. A good housekeeper will know the wisdom of it at once. Keep your plain accounts exact, that the household may be well ordered and no shilling go astray.

Keep a richer book beside it, in which the day is remembered as it felt and not merely as it cost. The town has argued whether the poor man was sound in his wits. Sensible people will not trouble over the question. They will do as he did, and be the richer for holding two records of one life — the one for good management, the other for a grateful heart.
