Dear Sir, — You will be diverted to hear of a discovery lately made in our parish, for the old logbook of the lighthouse upon the point, kept faithfully these forty years by a single hand, has been found to contain not one record of each night but two, laid down one beneath the other in the very same careful ink. In the first the keeper writes as his office required, noting the quarter of the wind, the trimming of the wick, and the passage of such vessels as the dark allowed him to descry; in the second he writes as a man alone with the sea will write, giving to the fog a disposition and to the tide a kind of speech, until the plain weather of the coast is transfigured into something very like a psalm. Our neighbours are much divided upon the meaning of it. Some among them, of a practical turn, esteem the second column an idleness unbecoming a public servant; others, and I confess myself of their number, find in it the natural overflow of a mind long confined to the company of gulls and lamplight.

I shall send you a fuller transcript when the sexton has done copying. It is a gentle wonder, and I would not have it lost.
